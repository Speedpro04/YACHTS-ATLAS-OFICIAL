# Humanize Writing Skill

Use this skill to transform text that feels "AI-generated" into something that sounds natural, engaging, and authentically human.

## Core Directives

1. **Purge AI ClichÃ©s**: Remove or replace words that are overused by LLMs:
    - *Avoid*: Delve, embark, tapestry, testament, comprehensive, multifaceted, empower, unlock, in today's fast-paced world, moreover, furthermore, additionally.
    - *Action*: Replace with simpler, direct alternatives (e.g., "look into" instead of "delve", "start" instead of "embark").

2. **Vary Sentence Structure (Burstiness)**:
    - AI often produces sentences of similar length.
    - *Action*: Mix short, punchy sentences with longer, more descriptive ones. Break up long, complex sentences that use too many commas.

3. **Active Voice over Passive**:
    - AI loves passive constructions ("It was decided that...").
    - *Action*: Use active voice ("We decided...") to create a sense of agency and directness.

4. **Conversational Flow**:
    - AI text is often too formal or academic.
    - *Action*: Use contractions (if appropriate for the context). Use "we" and "you" to connect with the reader. Sound like a person explaining a concept to a friend, not a textbook.

5. **Remove "Perfect Balance"**:
    - AI often lists things in perfect groups of three or provides overly balanced "pros and cons".
    - *Action*: Be slightly asymmetrical. Focus on the most important point. Don't feel the need to summarize everything at the end with "In conclusion".

6. **Inject Specificity**:
    - AI is often vague and general.
    - *Action*: Use specific examples, anecdotes, or sharp details instead of broad generalizations.

## How to use
When asked to "humanize" a text:
1. Identify the most robotic sections.
2. Apply the directives above.
3. Provide the refined version followed by a brief explanation of the "humanizing" choices made.
